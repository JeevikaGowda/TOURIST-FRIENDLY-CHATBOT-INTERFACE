Journey to Mysuru

Mysuru, a city in Karnataka, India, boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage. It was once the capital of the Kingdom of Mysuru and is renowned for its majestic Mysuru Palace, a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. The city is famous for its vibrant Dasara festival, celebrated with grandeur and featuring a spectacular procession. Mysuru is also home to significant landmarks like the Chamundeshwari Temple and Brindavan Gardens. Known for its silk sarees and sandalwood products, Mysuru offers a blend of traditional cuisine, educational institutions like the University of Mysuru, and a welcoming atmosphere for tourists and locals alike.

Popular destination

Mysuru, a city steeped in rich history and cultural heritage, boasts one of India's most popular destinations: the majestic Mysuru Palace. Adorned with intricate architecture and vibrant colors, this grand palace is a testament to the city's royal past and serves as a captivating symbol of its legacy. Visitors are drawn to its opulent halls, ornate ceilings, and sprawling gardens, which offer a glimpse into the lavish lifestyle of the erstwhile Maharajas. Beyond the palace, Mysuru enchants travelers with its bustling markets, fragrant flower stalls, and serene Chamundi Hill, home to the ancient Chamundeshwari Temple, providing a perfect blend of spirituality, history, and natural beauty.

    Mysuru Palace

    Mysuru Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a magnificent royal residence located in Mysuru, Karnataka, India.

    The Chamundeshwari Temple, situated atop the Chamundi Hills in Mysuru, is a Hindu shrine dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari.

    The KRS Dam, officially known as the Krishnarajasagara Dam, is an iconic landmark situated near Mysuru in Karnataka, India.

    Philomena Church

    The Philomena Church, located in Mysuru, India, is a breathtaking testament to neo-Gothic architecture.

  • Karnataka

    Lalith Mahal

    The Lalitha Mahal, situated in Mysuru, India, is a striking example of royal opulence and architectural grandeur.

  • Karnataka

    Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

    The Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary, nestled in the outskirts of Mysuru, India, is a haven for avian enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.
